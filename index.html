About Me

I am currently a Masters student in the University of British Columbia’s Master of Geomatics for Environmental Management (MGEM). I was born in New Mexico, USA where I grew up hiking, camping, and river running. I graduated from UBC with a bachelor’s degree in International Relations in 2021 which deepened my passion for global sustainable development. Between my undergraduate degree and MGEM, I worked as a wilderness guide in Colorado and as a legal assistant at a small law firm in Vancouver. I aim to leverage skills and knowledge from MGEM to pursue a career in disaster planning, mitigation, and relief, contributing to environmentally responsible and humane solutions. Outside of my school and work, I enjoy hiking, knitting, and spending time with my dog.

Capstone

This project develops a method for detecting forest cover change in Tajikistan's Pamir Mountains using historical Hexagon panchromatic imagery and modern PlanetScope multispectral data. The workflow is designed to be scalable for broader landscape analysis.
