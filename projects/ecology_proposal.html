Leveraging Sentinel Imagery to Model Aboveground Biomass and Estimate Carbon in Tajikistan's Pamir Mountains

In this course, we developed a proposal for a project exploring a topic in landscape ecology. My proposal extends my capstone project about historic forest extent in Tajikistan’s Pamir Mountains by addressing the need to quantify modern reforestation efforts in the region following decades of landscape degradation and subsequent reforestation efforts. Using Sentinel imagery and predictive modeling, the project intends to detect forest cover changes, estimate aboveground biomass (AGB), and predict the impact of reforestation on carbon sequestration.

The proposed research builds on my capstone project, which utilized Keyhole imagery, by connecting it to a topic in landscape ecology, demonstrating how historical and contemporary remote sensing can inform ecological restoration and resilience in high-elevation ecosystems. My proposal situates the proposed research in the context of decades of landscape degradation and internationally funded reforestation efforts in Tajikistan.

Objectives

  • Develop a clear and actionable research framework using accessible data.
  • Integrate interdisciplinary methods, including remote sensing, ecological modeling, and spatial analysis.
  • Demonstrate the significance of reforestation in carbon sequestration and landscape resilience.

Approach

  • Contextual Framing: Situate the research within a global restoration initiative (Bonn Challenge) while addressing local ecological and social dynamics.
  • Structured Methodology: Outline step-by-step processes for data collection, analysis, and interpretation.
  • Clear Communication: Use visuals (e.g., flowcharts and diagrams) to enhance understanding of methods and expected outcomes.
  • Future-Oriented Thinking: Predict the implications of the study’s findings for policy and funding decisions.
