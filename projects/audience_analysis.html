Audience Analysis for BC Ministry of Forests

Understanding the Communication Needs of Ministry Employees

This analysis focuses on identifying the key priorities of the BC Ministry of Forests' employees, who balance economic, environmental, and social concerns in their decision-making. The analysis aims to tailor communication strategies that address these priorities effectively, ensuring alignment with the Ministry's goals of sustainable forest management, reconciliation with Indigenous groups, and the long-term benefits of the Community Forestry Agreement.

Objectives

  • Identify key concerns and priorities of Ministry employees, including economic, environmental, and social factors.
  • Analyze the Ministry's decision-making framework to understand how these priorities influence policy and communication strategies.
  • Develop tailored messaging that aligns with the Ministry's goals, emphasizing measurable results and long-term impacts.

Approach

  • Conduct research on the Ministry's structure, key decision-makers, and their areas of focus.
  • Analyze the Ministry’s role in balancing forestry industry profitability with environmental protection and reconciliation.
  • Design communication strategies that incorporate data-driven approaches and values-based messaging.
  • Ensure messaging aligns with both institutional goals and the personal priorities of Ministry employees and key decision-makers.

Results

The result of this analysis is a tailored communication strategy that highlights the Ministry’s priorities in a way that aligns with their decision-making framework. The strategy emphasizes economic benefits, environmental sustainability, and social inclusivity, framing the renewal of the Community Forestry Agreement as a long-term, forward-looking decision that benefits all stakeholders.
