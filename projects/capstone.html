Capstone Project

Forest Cover Change Using Declassified Reconnaisance Imagery

This project investigates forest cover dynamics in Tajikistan's Pamir Mountains, focusing on how the Tajik Civil War impacted forest ecosystems. The goal of this project is to establish a method for forest extent and change detection that can then be scaled up to the broader study area. The project utilizes panchromatic imagery from the United Statesâ€™ Hexagon reconnaissance satellite program for historical analysis and modern multispectral imagery from PlanetScope for current assessments.

Objectives

  • Establish a method for classifying Hexagon panchromatic imagery to identify forest extent in the late 1970s.
  • Map forest cover change between late 1970s and present.
  • Explore the potential of historic remote sensing data in understanding the effects of conflict on landscapes.

Approach

  • Panchromatic Image Classification: A hybrid segmented and pixel-based methodology is used to identify forested areas in panchromatic images from the Hexagon satellite missions, with a focus on texture metrics to distinguish tree cover from other land types.
  • Multispectral Image Classification: Modern multispectral imagery from Planet Labs covering the same area as the panchromatic imagery is classified to compare forest extent.
  • Change Detection and Analysis: Difference rasters and landscape metrics are used to quantify and map changes in forest extent.

Challenges and Limitations

Due to data availability challenges, I only had access to part of a single strip of a Hexagon image that was orthorectified and ready for analysis. Instead of attempting to quantify forest change across a larger area of interest, I focussed my project on developing a workflow for analysis that can be scaled up to more imagery.

Impact and Future Direction

This project contributes to the broader understanding of how environmental change in conflict zones can be monitored through remote sensing. It also highlights the importance of historical context in interpreting environmental data. The project can be expanded by analyzing larger and varied regions that also experience environmental changes on decadal scales where high resolution panchromatic imagery from the Hexagon program could be used to deepen our understanding of the environment and its processes.
