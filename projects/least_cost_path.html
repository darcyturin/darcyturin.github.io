Least Cost Path Modeling

Raster Analysis and Least Cost Path Modeling for Grizzly Bear Habitat in Alberta

This lab focused on raster operations using the Geospatial Data Abstraction Library (GDAL), command-line tools, QGIS, and ArcGIS Pro. We processed raster datasets to create a cost raster representing the cost of movement for a grizzly bear within the Yellowhead Bear Management Area in Alberta. The analysis explored how landscape features and human activity influence the movement and challenges faced by the bears.

Objectives

  • Using different sources, derive new rasters through raster algebra and cost assignment.
  • Combine these derivative rasters into a cost raster by weighting slope, land cover, and road distance.
  • Perform a least cost path analysis on the cost raster to model an example of a grizzly bear route.

Approach

  • Mosaic input rasters and clip to the area of interest.
  • Reclassify land cover raster by assigning cost values.
  • Create derivative rasters for slope, land cover, and road distance.
  • Generate cost raster by assigning weights to slope, land cover, and road distance.
  • Model least cost path by making accumulated distance and backlink layers and applying the optimal path as a line tool in ArcGIS.

Results

The final product for this lab is a map of a least cost path from the centroid of the primary habitat to a random point in the secondary habitat. This map illustrates an example of the impact of different cost factors on a final path, demonstrating the power of raster analysis in addressing conservation challenges.
