Exploring Place Through Literature and Mapping

During my undergraduate studies, I took a course that explored "sense of place" in Russian literature, different interpretations and experiences of St. Petersburg through a variety of media such as books, poetry, and film. One assignment was to use ArcGIS StoryMaps to interpret the spatial narrative of a selected work.
For my project, I mapped locations mentioned in Nikolai Gogolâ€™s short story "The Nose." This analysis reveals an emphasis on wealthy neighbourhoods and industrial areas, but a lack of focus on the experiences of the working class in St. Petersburg. Although this project lies outside traditional geomatics, it furthered my understanding of mapping as a powerful tool for interpretation and communication, and uncovered story elements that may have been hidden otherwise.
