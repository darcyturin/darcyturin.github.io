Supervised Image Classification

This lab focused on supervised image classification of a Landsat 9 image of the Southern Gulf Islands, BC. We delineated training polygons in QGIS and then used the RSToolbox in R to perform a maximum likelihood supervised classification and assess the accuracy of the classification.

Objectives

  • Gain practical experience in supervised image classification using a Landsat image.
  • Prepare training data, perform classification, and assess the accuracy of the resulting land cover map.

Method

  • Training data selection:
    • Delineate polygons representing seven land cover classes (Broadleaf Forest, Coniferous Forest, Exposed Soil and Rocks, High Density Developed Areas, Low Density Developed Areas, Non-Forest Vegetation, Water).
    • Ensure sufficient polygon coverage for each class, considering spatial variability within each class.
    • Split the polygon data into training and validation sets.
  • Employ the Maximum Likelihood classification algorithm to classify the Landsat image based on the spectral signatures of the training data.
  • Evaluate the accuracy (overall, user's, producer's) of the classification using a confusion matrix.

Results

  • Produced a classified land cover map of the study area.
  • Assessed the accuracy of the classification using the calculated metrics.
  • Identified potential sources of error and areas for improvement in the classification process.
