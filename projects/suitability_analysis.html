Suitability Analysis for Humpback Whale Habitat in Hawaii

This lab focused on using GIS to execute a marine spatial planning exercise. Through suitability analysis, we identified the best areas to expand the Hawaiian Islands Humpback Whale National Marine Sanctuary. The analysis considered the conflicts between marine habitats and human activities to prioritize areas most in need of protection.

Objectives

  • Understand the principles of suitability analysis in GIS.
  • Use geospatial tools to model and rank areas based on conservation suitability indices.

Approach

  • Suitability Analysis: Conduct ordinal classification to assign suitability scores, considering the following factors:
    • Area Size: Larger areas scored higher in suitability.
    • Cetacean Sightings: Areas with more cetacean sightings were ranked higher.
    • Ocean Use Intensity: Areas with more human uses ranked higher in suitability to reflect the increased need for protection.
  • Geospatial Operations:
    • Perform vector polygon overlays and database table intersections.
    • Integrate data layers to develop a suitability index ranging from 0 (unsuitable) to 1 (highly suitable).

Results

I designed a map identifying the largest, most suitable areas for sanctuary expansion to reduce whale strikes and ensure long-term habitat protection. The analysis balances habitat protection and human ocean use, demonstrating the role of GIS in transparent and informed marine spatial planning.
